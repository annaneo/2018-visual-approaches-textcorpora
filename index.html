Regesta Imperii

Visual analysis of relations and visual exploration of the corpus

Regesta Imperii

Edition of charters of the Holy Roman Kings (751-1519)

  • Since 1829
  • Originally: preliminary work to the "Monumenta Germaniae Historica"
  • Over 180,000 sources are available online
  • 100 print publications
  • Since 2001 online
  • Since 2007: unrestricted, searchable full-text database with possibility of direct user feedback
  • Research data (CEI) licensed with CC BY
  • RI plus: regional charters
  • International interdisciplinary medieval literature database: RI OPAC (2 Million entries)
 http://www.regesta-imperii.de/

Regesta Imperii

Graph databases*

  • Key concept: data modelled as relationships (edges) between entities (nodes)
  • Entities are directly related in the store
  • Intuitive modelling
  • Easy retrieval of relationships
  • Relations between persons
  • Acts described in regestae
  • Relations between regesta and registers
  • Citation networks
* Paper and Data: Andreas Kuczera: Graphdatenbanken für Historiker | Graph database Regesta Imperii
